Zscaler warns of phishing campaign abusing ZIA authentication pages

Zscaler has issued an advisory warning customers about a phishing campaign that clones Zscaler Internet Access authentication pages to harvest corporate credentials from remote workers.

The campaign sends emails impersonating IT helpdesk notifications, directing employees to lookalike domains that mimic the ZIA login flow. Captured credentials are relayed in real time, allowing attackers to establish authenticated sessions before tokens expire.

Zscaler recommends enabling phishing-resistant multi-factor authentication and configuring conditional access policies that reject logins originating from unmanaged devices.

The company said fewer than fifty customers were targeted in the initial wave, and affected organizations have been notified directly.
