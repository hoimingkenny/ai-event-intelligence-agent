CyberArk vault flaw now under mass exploitation, ransomware gangs join in

The recently disclosed CyberArk Privileged Access Manager vulnerability CVE-2026-21001 is now being exploited at scale, with ransomware affiliates joining the initial wave of targeted attacks, according to incident responders.

Scanning activity targeting the vault authentication bypass has increased tenfold since the emergency patch was released, and honeypot data shows automated exploitation kits are now circulating on underground forums.

At least two ransomware groups have incorporated the exploit into their playbooks, using stolen privileged credentials to disable endpoint protection and deploy encryptors across entire domains within hours of initial compromise.

Incident responders emphasize that patching alone is insufficient for organizations that were exposed before applying the update: all credentials stored in affected vaults must be rotated, and audit logs should be reviewed for unauthorized retrievals dating back at least two weeks.
